In this paper, we present a comprehensive evaluation of post-training quantization (PTQ) techniques for audio DiTs, analyzing the trade-offs between static and dynamic quantization schemes. We explore two practical extensions (1) a denoising-timestep-aware smoothing method that adapts quantization scales per-input-channel and timestep to mitigate activation outliers, and (2) a lightweight low-rank adapter (LoRA)-based branch derived from singular value decomposition (SVD) to compensate for residual weight errors. Using Stable Audio Open we benchmark W8A8 and W4A8 configurations across objective metrics and human perceptual ratings. Our results show that dynamic quantization preserves fidelity even at lower precision, while static methods remain competitive with lower latency. Overall, our findings show that low-precision DiTs can retain high-fidelity generation while reducing memory usage by up to 79%.
